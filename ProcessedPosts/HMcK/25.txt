As I went out on a more regular basis, I realized that the world wasn\u2019t as scary as I thought it would be.\u00a0 If I was being laughed at and pointed towards, I didn\u2019t notice it.\u00a0 People were kind to me.\u00a0 People complimented my outfit or the skill I had when it came to walking in heels.\u00a0 My confidence grew.\u00a0 My self-esteem grew.\u00a0 Blending in, in those early days, gave me an opportunity to experience life as incognito as possible, considering I am a six foot tall (and taller in heels) transgirl. A LOT of flowers.   I remember the day I wore the outfit pictured above. I was feeling particularly bold that afternoon and opted for a bright, tight pink dress with matching pink heels.\u00a0 I looked at myself in every mirror I saw at the mall that day and marveled how it didn\u2019t seem that long ago when I tried so hard to blend in, to not be noticed.\u00a0 This outfit was about as far away as incognito as one could get. These days I no longer try to blend in.\u00a0 There\u2019s not much in my closet that I am not comfortable wearing.\u00a0 Of course, I still believe in dressing appropriately, I am not going to wear my leather dress and five inch stilettos to the grocery store, for example.
