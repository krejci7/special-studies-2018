Here are the things that I most often tell them: Chill out and give it some time.\u00a0 My husband and I have been there, in that early, panicked rush to figure out what was going on with our son and if he was going through a phase or if his behaviors had some deeper meaning.\u00a0 The only way to tell if something is a phase is to wait it out and patiently observe it.\u00a0 If the behaviors go on for an extended period of time your child may be gender nonconforming.\u00a0 My son has definitely taught me patience. Gather a stellar supporting cast.\u00a0 Like any family raising a child with special or unique needs, you\u2019ll benefit from help.\u00a0 We\u2019d be lost without our family, friends, pediatrician, therapist and child advocate.\u00a0 Children like C.J. have the highest rate of suicide attempts in the world and are much more likely to suffer from major depressions, substance abuse and unsafe sexual behaviors.\u00a0 To raise a healthy gender nonconforming child, it\u2019s going to take more than one or two people.
