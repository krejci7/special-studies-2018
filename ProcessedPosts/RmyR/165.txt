My Notes: It takes a family to launch a gender nonconforming child\u2026but, that\u2019s not necessarily what a sibling wants to do or how they want to spend their time.\u00a0 Are we asking siblings to step outside their comfort zone and/or do too much? Two important jobs of a parent are to help the child be their authentic self and to keep them safe.\u00a0 When you are raising a gender diverse child, sometimes it feels like those two jobs are at odds. Siblings are an often forgotten \u2014 but critical \u2014 part of the family support system. They outlive the parents.\u00a0 They are part of the same generation. \u00a0\u00a0And, they need support too. Siblings can be that child\u2019s best ally or worse foe, depending on the circumstances. Sometimes the sibling has the ability to \u201cout\u201d their gender diverse sibling.\u00a0 They hold a lot of power.\u00a0 What will they do with that power?\u00a0 Are they being asked to keep a huge family secret? \u00a0Is that fair? Siblings typically want to help, but they don\u2019t know what to do or say and that can lead them to feel inadequate. Siblings can grow resentful of all the focus on gender. What do siblings need?  Empathy about their own anxieties, confusions and vulnerabilities. A place where they can air their own feelings out independently.\u00a0 They need their own space and often don\u2019t get it. A dialogue between only the siblings (with an adult as a listener).\u00a0 The siblings talk honestly.\u00a0 How does it feel to be gender diverse?\u00a0 How does it feel to have a gender diverse sibling?
