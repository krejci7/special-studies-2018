You acknowledge that the right wing and TERFs are looking for these stories but then you hand them to them on a silver platter right at the moment they need them. It is like you are trying to play Devil\u2019s Advocate at a time when the devil doesn\u2019t need any help. Those are very real stories that deserve to be told\u00a0in the right time and place with the appropriate level of nuance.  But that is not how you approached this story. You erased the nuance of the various levels of social and medical transition and largely treated it as a binary problem. You cite concerns about trans youth but don\u2019t mention hormone blockers until almost the end or really discuss why having the option to postpone that decision is so helpful. You also don\u2019t treat gender as a journey but rather a yes or no decision. As a genderqueer blogger who writes on the topic (https://beardedgenderqueer.wordpress.com/) I know that that kind of nuance is possible and if you had a trans person authentically consulting on this article, or better yet, as the writer, you would know that. Your treatment of the \u201cother side\u201d of transition as this huge problem is on the same level of tone-deafness as treating anti-vaxxers or climate change deniers like they are on equal footing as science in the name of \u201cneutral journalism.\u201d Just because there are dissenters doesn\u2019t mean they hold the same footing as widely proven facts. And you gloss over the fact that it is the discrimination that we face that prompts most of this reversion.  For you to publish this article during Pride Month is also particularly hurtful. These are objectively people who are not proud of their identity, or if they are it is essentially a \u201ccisgender pride\u201d story which I hope you know is unacceptable during June. You are amplifying the concerns of cis people who want more gatekeeping over the voices of trans people during our own month. Plus your ending makes it sound like everyone could solve dysphoria by being more connected to their body. I know that you know better than to do this. Katie and others on your team spoke with Danni Askini at\u00a0Gender Justice League\u00a0[local trans leader and professional activist] before you published the article and she expressed concern about the tone and timing but yet you still chose to publish it this month and it sounds like you lied to her about it as well.
