Once these roles are established, they make it easier to segregate other roles. If women are taking care of infants, it\u2019s easy for them to keep caring for older children, and eventually for the elderly and sick. If childcare happens mostly in the home, it makes sense for women to take care of other household needs. If men are fighting, it makes sense for them to be policing internal order, and then it\u2019s easy for them to be the ones who set the internal order.  If they\u2019re smashing and lifting, it makes sense for them to build houses and fortifications, and then it\u2019s easy for them to be the ones who make machines. Some women are big and strong, and some men are small and weak. Technological and social changes have made a lot of these biological generalizations irrelevant. The US Army\u2019s recent official inclusion of women in combat is notable, but women have been fighting throughout human history.  There have been women leaders and male nurturers. There will probably always be roles in every society that are more strongly associated with one gender or the other.
