If you are from the North Shore of Massachusetts you are probably rolling your eyes because\u00a0Woodman\u2019s is a tourist trap. Everyone has their favorite clam shack or place to go for lobster in the rough. There are about a dozen places to choose from between Rockport and Ipswich. Woodman\u2019s is a treat\u00a0for me,\u00a0and everything I ate there was delicious. Once you get over the prices there is a lot to enjoy ($30 for a humongous plate of fried clams, homemade french fries, and onion rings). Woodman\u2019s is chaotic and confusing if you don\u2019t know how it works. It is huge. It is self-service. There are three stations, each with their own line (one just for whole lobsters, one for drinks (in my case beer), and one for fried clams,\u00a0fried\u00a0scallops, fried haddock, french fries, onion rings, lobster rolls, clam fritters, corn, and cole slaw). An adult gull will consume over\u00a020% of its body weight everyday in food. I think I came close to that at Woodman\u2019s. Unlike the gulls, I didn\u2019t \u00a0have to go\u00a0dumpster diving or eat off of someone else\u2019s plate. In the water I felt like a buoy. Or at least I felt buoyant. Bouncy and happy. I brought all of my swimming costume paraphernalia with me. The first day at the beach I wore board shorts that hit just above the knees (commando) and a loose-fitting short sleeve rash guard (bottom loop of shirt tied into the fly lacing of the shorts). I did notice a handful of other people like me wearing various configurations of trunks and tops. There were also a lot of good-looking guys wearing board shorts and rash guards. They didn\u2019t look self-conscious either. I am a convert. I enjoyed being back at the beach. The second day I wore shorter trunks and another rash guard, and had a similar experience. I never wore the \u201cbike short\u201d swim bottoms or the swimming tank tops (which I perceive as slightly less masculine).
