a couple are to the film\u2019s website directly.\u00a0 I highly recommend the first 6, and then I don\u2019t recommend the 7th at all.Suicide Kale \u2013 This film was shot in 5 days, with no budget.\u00a0 Even without stipulating that, it\u2019s an intriguing and complex look into the dynamics between 2 lesbian couples at a lunch party, and what happens when one of them finds a suicide note tucked away somewhere.\u00a0 Sounds like it\u2019d be intense, but it\u2019s actually fairly lighthearted and humorous.\u00a0 Very well attended, mostly women in the audience.Paris 05:59: Theo and Hugo \u2013 This film takes place in real time, as two men fall for each other in a love/lust at first sight type-of-way.\u00a0 Things start out in a laid back, sexually explicit environment / vibe, but quickly become complicated as the two grapple with an emotional twist, and whether the connection is worth working through it.\u00a0 Super well attended; roughly 98% men, 2% women.Closet Monster \u2013 This might have been my favorite one this year.\u00a0 It\u2019s a coming-of-age story of an 18-year old boy coming to terms with his gay identity, complicated by flashbacks of a hate crime he witnessed as a young child.\u00a0 There were body horror elements to the film, which I was really into.\u00a0 Plus, Isabella Rossellini\u2019s voice is featured as a talking pet hamster!\u00a0 Well attended, very diverse audience, including young adults, which isn\u2019t usually the case.Strike A Pose \u2013 This also was possibly my favorite one this year.\u00a0 It\u2019s a documentary about the 7 dancers who went on tour with Madonna in 1990, and where they\u2019re at now.\u00a0 Not all of them are gay!\u00a0 Some of them had a lot of secrets at the time, and they are now much more open about things.\u00a0 Way more emotional than I would have thought, going into the film.\u00a0 Very well attended, diverse audience but definitely mostly men.Girls Lost \u2013 A film from Finland.\u00a0 Magical realism.\u00a0 3 tight-knit female friends, about 14 years old, are frequently bullied at school but find solace in each other and a greenhouse they regularly retreat to.\u00a0 Suddenly, a magical flower appears; they drink the nectar and turn into boys, for about 12 hours.\u00a0 One of them realizes he is transgender, and he becomes addicted to the feelings the nectar brings.\u00a0 At the same time, he is self-destructive, in an effort to understand himself.\u00a0 Really well attended, diverse audience.Real Boy \u2013 This is a documentary spanning over a few years, as a singer/songwriter FTM person starts his transition.\u00a0 It focuses on the relationship between him and his mom, between him and a best friend who is also trans, and between him and another trans singer/songwriter who is a little older and acts as a mentor of sorts.\u00a0 Issues that are discussed:\u00a0 hormones, sobriety, top surgery, family dynamics.\u00a0 Also really well attended, diverse audience including young adults, which is not generally the case.Lovesong \u2013 This was a flop.\u00a0 My spouse and I were attracted to it partly because it stars Jena Malone, and also it seemed like a complex story-line.\u00a0 It was fairly complex, but they characters were not at all likeable.\u00a0 Two female friends who go way back, dabble in acting on their attraction to each other, at various points spanning a few years.\u00a0 Kinda boring.\u00a0 Sparsely attended, mostly women.My spouse and I diligently filled out audience participation ballots for each film, and a survey about the festival in general.\u00a0 My most important feedback:\u00a0 more films with trans characters please.\u00a0 And also, please make it more affordable.\u00a0", "feedtitle": "Page 6 \u2013 janitorqueer"}
