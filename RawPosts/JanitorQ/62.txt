Written by Rhiannon Robear; reprinted with permission.One night this summer, I was at the gay club looking glam, and having a smoke break outside with my friends.\u00a0\u00a0 A cis gay guy came up to us and started talking about trans things in that \u201cyou\u2019re a visibly trans and/or gender non-conforming person so I\u2019m about to lay down all my trans knowledge, thoughts, and critiques for you\u201d kind of way (a.k.a. completely unasked/unwanted).\u00a0 Overall it was a real drag, and I brushed him off mostly, but then he held my hands and looked me in the eyes and said, \u201cbaby, I know you\u2019re trying to be the belle of the ball, but the reality is you\u2019re built like a 6 foot amazon linebacker, and you need to work that.\u201d\u00a0 I was taken aback like where the fuck do you get off telling me who I am and what I should do.\u00a0 But as much as I hate entertaining cis-notions of what trans people are or should be, what he said was true, and deep inside me I knew I felt that and it was the first time someone told me that I could &should be a woman on my own terms.The reality is:\u00a0 I\u2019m 5\u201911, probably between 250-300 pounds, hairy as all hell, and I wear size 13 women\u2019s shoes:\u00a0 I\u2019m a big girl.\u00a0 I spent years of my life identifying as a gay man, and trying to work at accepting and loving my body & myself in a culture that taught me that being fat & being femme made me undesireable, unattractive, and inferior.\u00a0 It took me YEARS to be comfortable with who I am, and that process has changed me, and how I value myself \u2013 simply put:\u00a0 I don\u2019t do things for other people anymore, I do things for myself.I identified as non-binary for the past two years, and over this time, I\u2019ve slowly began to come into myself as a woman, and I\u2019m currently in the process of coming out as a transgender woman.\u00a0 It\u2019s very exciting and liberating and I\u2019m now out at work and am \u2018test driving\u2019 my new name and pronouns.\u00a0 This being said, what I am most dreading about coming out isn\u2019t being faced with disapproval or abandonment (I am privileged with supportive family and friends), but more about those in my life forcing feminine ideals upon me when I start to identify as a woman and not strictly non-binary.In a perfect world, would I like to wear a full face of make-up, have minimal to no body hair, have a feminine physique, and be read 100% of the time as a woman?\u00a0 \u2013 SURE!\u00a0 But the reality is, I work two jobs, I\u2019m a full time student, and I\u2019m involved in a couple different organizations, and I don\u2019t have time for that.\u00a0 My emotional well-being is like, \u201cyou work at 8am, you don\u2019t have time to put your face on for an hour every morning,\u201d \u201cyou literally can\u2019t even reach your back hair, how are you supposed to regularly keep that shaved,\u201d etc.\u00a0 Luckily for me, I think that the resilience I learned as a fat & femme gay man allows me to be comfortable in my own skin regardless of others\u2019 perceptions.\u00a0 I also recognize the privilege of being comfortable enough with myself & my gender to not be dysphoric to an incapacitating extent wherein I need to hold my body to a standard for public consumption.Why yes!\u00a0 I AM a woman with a hairy back \u2013 if it bothers you I\u2019ll hand you a razor and you can shave it for me!\u00a0 Until then please fuck off with your gendered policing and let me live my life on my terms.
