agendas surrounding this experience.\u00a0 In theory, I am on board with all I\u2019ve seen (and let me clarify that what I\u2019ve seen is almost entirely online at this time, and not reflected in There has been a push for more gender neutral bathrooms in public places, over the past few years, particularly at schools and on campuses.\u00a0 Why schools and campuses?\u00a0 I\u2019m not sure exactly, but I can make an educated guess.\u00a0 People in their teens and early twenties are at these places en masse.\u00a0 People in their teens and early twenties tend to be going through changes \u2013 they may be focusing on their identities (including gender identities) more so than the general population, so it makes sense they would want to change the spaces where they spend the most time, in order to feel more safe and comfortable.I hope this movement spreads beyond schools, to include government buildings, corporate chains, every place, really.\u00a0 I think that it will, or, at least, I think this agenda will gain more traction than the push for gender neutral pronouns, which is, comparatively speaking, somewhat nebulous.\u00a0 changes in language are more about changing people\u2019s perceptions and notions on a large scale (potentially very difficult).\u00a0 Bathrooms are about physical spaces, with a direct request that involves a straightforward solution.New buildings can go up with this floor plan in mind, without much more money or labor.\u00a0 Existing buildings can be remodeled and reorganized.\u00a0 (Something that happens frequently anyway.)\u00a0 Often it\u2019s just a matter of relabeling existing layouts (at no additional cost).\u00a0 For example, if a restaurant has a single stall restroom for men, and a single stall restroom for women, how much work would it take to get that restaurant to just change them both to gender neutral bathrooms?\u00a0 Hopefully within the near future, not much convincing work at all!Until this is happening anywhere and everywhere (I hope I see the day!!!!), here is an amazing website resource:\u00a0 Refugee Restrooms.All you have to do is type in your city or location, and it is a database that lists
